About Me

This is who I am. This is what I do.

My name is Caitlin Stanton and I'm more than a needle in a pile of needles.

Okay, that was a necessary cliche, so don't judge me.

All I'm saying is, I try to see myself as a non-ordinary high school girl. At least, if you're basing ordinary high school girls on the movie stereotypes.

Except for the not-having-any-free-time thing. That's totally me.

I am a high school senior from New York who spends most of her time at school or in her pajamas in front of a computer. Though this passion for technology really started through watching Shane Dawson and Danisnotonfire videos on YouTube, my time spent in the Girls Who Code summer program last year only strengthened my curiosity in the world wide weirdness that is the Internet. It is and always will be my one true love [sorry Gerard Way] <3 I can be found coding and #winning at hackathons around the city, practicing her pitching on the softball mound, or curled in the fetal position reading a John Green novel. I am a true fangirl, and not afraid to show it, whether it's by listening to MCR albums from ten years ago or liking all of my fave YouTubers' Instagram photos. I am now getting the chance to see the worlds of coding and fangirling collide as a Cambio #BUILTBYGIRLS intern.

WOOT WOOT *fist pump*

Coding

It all started sophomore year. We're required to take at least one semester of an Intro to Computer Science course at my school, and I ended up taking it spring semester. It was then that I learned the basics of Dr.Racket and NetLogo, introducing me to the world of coding.

I continued this into the summer with my six weeks at Girls Who Code. Long story short, it changed my life.

My life basically revolves around coding now, whether it's at school taking computer science courses, participating in hackathons, and making my own stuff (hackathons, websites, etc.).

The fact that I can make whatever I want with a couple keystrokes is just, liberating.

Softball

I didn't always play softball.

I started out on a co-ed baseball team when I was six. I dedicated my entire elementary-school-self to fielding grounders, catching popups, and hitting homeruns. The only problem, something I didn't recognize until I was maybe ten, was that I was surrounded by boys.

Trust me, that would be awesome now, but being the one of the only two girls on a co-ed wasn't that fun.

I kicked all their asses, to be frank :P

Therefore, when I was ten, my parents switched me over to the softball section of my league, and it all clicked into place. I got into pitching, learning how to throw fastballs and changeups (that admittedly look the same, exactly) with the windmill motion. I graduated from wanting to walk every at-bat to eventually hitting an over-the-fence homerun (best. feeling. EVAR). I ended up joining two teams, my high school's varisty team and sticking with my first league.

I'm gonna be honest with myself--I'm probably not the best softball player out there. Not by a long shot. I probably don't have too much of a chance of getting onto a Division 1 varsity team in college.

But that's not going to stop me from trying to get my team past the first round of the championships (this is the year I CAN FEEL IT).

Reading

When I was little, my parents would shut me up by shoving a book at me. Since then, I've embraced my true antisocial nature by bringing a book wherever I go (whenever I go outside).

Legitimately, everywhere.

Every time I read a book, my mind does this weird thing where it basically turns into a TV screen and the book comes to life. I'm weird, what can I say? But it makes reading that much more fun-- I get to create my own little world, with me as the director.

I will literally read anything, as long as its good. That's the only criterion.

Although I have an extreme penchant for YA novels...Don't judge me.
